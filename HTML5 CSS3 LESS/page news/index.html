Cowboy

Names used in the Wild West of the USA in relation to the shepherd cattle. The era of the cowboys started from 1865, when it was necessary to drive away in a giant hijacking feral herds of cattle, primarily located in Texas. This era ended some twenty years later. About a third of the cowboys were Negroes gained their freedom after the Civil War, but had no job, no assets. More one-third of the cowboys were Mexicans, and a third - the descendants of immigrants from Europe. Cowboys worked on a ranch and cattle dealer engaged detour area ranch, repair fences, looking for stragglers from the herd of cows, branding calves, and many others. Initially grassland in the West were unfenced and often hosts various livestock grazing and mixed together. Therefore, every year had to make the corral and cattle section. Participation in such a pen required by cowboy considerable skill and it has grown from annual pens favorite entertainment cowboys - rodeo.

Mexican-Apache War

A series of conflicts between Spain and later Mexico and Arab tribes. These wars began in 1600, when the territory of today's New Mexico Spanish colonists arrived. The war between the Mexicans and Apaches was particularly intense in the period from 1831 to 1850. Subsequently, the Mexican clash with Apaches coincided with the Apache wars waged by the United States, such as the War of Victoria. Mexico continued fighting against hostile Apache bands until the end of 1915.The Spanish first encountered the Apache, whom they called Querechos, in 1541 in the Texas panhandle. At the time the Apache were buffalo hunting nomads who had trading relationships with the Pueblos of the Rio Grande valley. The early contacts were friendly, but in the 17th century, the relationship between Spaniard and Apache deteriorated because of slave raids by the Spaniards and Apache attacks on the Spanish and Pueblo settlements in New Mexico.
